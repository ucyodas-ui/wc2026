UC Master Yodas' World Cup Football 2026 Competition

Welcome to my World Cup 2026 competition. I hope you will enter.

There is not too much to it:

Predict who you think will win each stage of the world cup.

You will get points for how well you predict each stage.

Most points win.

What do you win?

Nothing.

Or I might draw you up a winners certificate if I'm up to it after watching a hundred games of football.

Why enter then?

For fun.

So do you want to have more fun?

Create your own mini-league with your mates and see who has the best football knowledge.

I'll display the leaders in each mini-league in the results.

You are allowed to be in multiple mini-leagues.

Can you help the Cobras?

We are helping to support the Canterbury Police Football team (Cobras) to go to The Australasian Emergency Services games.

If you can, please donate $2 to the Cobras when you enter. See more here.

Please note that all money goes to the Cobras trip. None of it goes to me at all.

Do I have to pay to enter?

No, it is free. Hence why there is no prize.

But any support for the Cobras is really really appreciated.

However, if you and your mates, team, or workmates etc, want to put some money on it, then use the mini-league feature. Make sure everyone knows the name of the league, and get them to add it on the entry form. I'll keep track of who is leading.

You'll have to deal with the money part yourself.

How do you score points?

Check this points chart. The better you predict the results, the more points you get.

Generally, based on past competitions, if you pick the actual winner of the world cup, and one of the other semi-finalists, you probably will win.

But what if I end up with the same points at the end?

Yeah, this happened once previously, so I've now have added a bonus tie-break question.

Pick the score in the final, and whoever is closest will win the tie-break.

How long does it take to enter?

Depends how seriously you want to take it. But for most of us it will take about 5 minutes.

Where do I find results?

In the past I emailed results each day but that was sort of a bit spammy for most people. So this time around I'll post all RESULTS HERE.

Who are the yodas?

The Yodas are a Masters football team playing for the University of Canterbury in New Zealand, many of whom have been playing together for 30 years.

Several of our members like donuts and also play for the Cobras - also known as the Canterbury Police Football team.
